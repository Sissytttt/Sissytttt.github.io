Descendant Of The Desert

“Descendant of The Desert” is a conceptual wearable device that intends to help humans survive under extreme conditions in deserts. It speculates a future where we human beings have to implement technical means to adapt to the desert-like future environment. We aim to present a new human biomorph that enables us to survive in the desert without taking refuge from the physical dependencies.

Key Words:
Wearable Interactive Installation, Speculative Design, Human Evolution

Sep 2022 - Dec 2022

Technology:
CircuitPython
Fabrication
3D printing
laser Cutting

Group Project
Jiapei Yao, Liuliu Zhu, Shiyuan Tian, Xiaoyan Zhou Role: Collaborate on Research and Concept Development, Final Fabrication and Assemble , Documentation and Filming. Specifically focused on Overall Design, Technical Implementation of Waist Motor and Spine Bending Movement, Video Design.

In a speculative future, where environmental degradation and technological advancements lead to the transformation of individuals into cyborgs, surviving in a desolate desert becomes a reality. Biomechanically and physiologically, "Descendant of the Desert" presents an Exoskeleton x Spine, integrating multiple sensors and actuators to enhance human perception and interaction with the environment. In collaboration with research and concept development, I am in charge of designing, sketching, and addressing the technical aspects, and also specifically focusing on implementing the waist motor and bringing the spine bending movement to realization. Assembled and tailored collaboratively, this potential human evolution sparks contemplation on the degradation of human sensory information and free will due to technological advancements, where machine algorithms may substitute human free will and decision-making.
